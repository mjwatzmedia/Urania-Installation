Overview

This guide documents the fabrication and setup of the physical components used in the current iteration of Urania. It focuses on the Pepper’s Ghost hologram stage and considerations for installing the work in a shared exhibition space.

This iteration does not include a projector loop or Raspberry Pi–based video playback. All instructions below reflect the installation as currently exhibited.

Pepper’s Ghost Stage · 3D TV Shell

The Pepper’s Ghost “hologram” uses a compact stage with an acrylic panel housed inside a TV-style shell. A smartphone placed face-up inside the structure reflects its image onto the acrylic, creating the illusion of a floating figure.

Files you’ll need:

  • 3D models for the TV shell (.stl or .3mf)
  • 3D models for the internal stage and phone holder
  • Dimensions for the clear acrylic panel

3D files for the illusion stage are available here:
Illusion Stage · 3D models on GitHub

Build Steps (Summary)

  1. 3D-print the TV shell and internal stage components. Assemble the shell.
  2. Cut the acrylic panel to size and mount it inside the shell at the correct angle.
  3. Test-fit the smartphone so the reflected image aligns cleanly in the acrylic.
  4. Adjust internal spacing and angle until the hologram appears centered and stable.

Installation Considerations

The Pepper’s Ghost stage is installed as part of a larger physical environment that includes sculptural elements, photographic prints, and data visualizations.

  • Control ambient light to preserve hologram contrast.
  • Place the stage at a comfortable viewing height for close inspection.
  • Ensure power and cable management remain visible, as these elements are conceptually significant.
  • Allow clear sightlines between the hologram and the surrounding sculptural assemblage.

The installation prioritizes transparency of materials and infrastructure rather than concealment.

Future Build Variants

Urania is designed as an evolving installation. While this documentation reflects the current exhibited iteration, future builds may explore alternative configurations or expanded components as the project develops.

  • Alternate hologram content
    Different holographic performances or behaviors rendered within the Pepper’s Ghost stage.
  • Expanded data layers
    Additional or updated visualizations reflecting changing regional energy or infrastructure conditions.
  • Time-based media elements
    Optional inclusion of projected or screen-based media in future exhibition contexts.
  • Scaled fabrication variants
    Adjustments to the stage or housing for different venues, budgets, or audience distances.

Any future variants will be documented and versioned separately. This guide describes the current iteration only.
