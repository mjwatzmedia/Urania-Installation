Urania

An interdisciplinary installation using holographic illusion, sculpture, and data visualization

Build & documentation: view project details →

Overview

This site documents the current iteration of Urania, an interdisciplinary installation that combines a Pepper’s Ghost hologram, sculptural assemblage, photographic prints, and regional energy data to examine the material and environmental costs of artificial intelligence.

Rather than presenting AI as an abstract or immaterial system, the work grounds computation in physical infrastructure, domestic space, and human labor.

Installation Components

  • Pepper’s Ghost hologram stage
    A smartphone-based holographic illusion using historical stage techniques to render Urania as a speculative AI figure.
  • Floor-based sculptural assemblage
    A found-object body constructed from clothing, electronics, cabling, and domestic materials, foregrounding the human cost of corporate computational systems.
  • Photographic prints
    Large-scale images referencing architectural, ecclesiastical, and infrastructural forms, treating data centers as contemporary sites of faith and extraction.
  • Data visualizations
    Simplified graphs derived from TEP/TEPC electricity-load data, situating AI infrastructure within Tucson’s regional grid.

About the Project

Urania operates at the intersection of speculative fiction, domestic infrastructure, and environmental accounting. The project treats AI not as a sentient agent, but as a corporate system sustained by continuous energy input, human attention, and material extraction.
