Overview

This site documents the current iteration of Urania, an interdisciplinary installation centered on a 3D-printed illusion monitor and paired photographic “smear” prints. The work combines a Pepper’s Ghost hologram stage, sculptural collage, and laser-cut acrylic framing to examine the social and environmental costs of artificial intelligence from the vantage point of Tucson.

Rather than presenting AI as an abstract or immaterial system, Urania ties computation to physical hardware, water-stressed desert infrastructure, and the way corporate narratives about “innovation” overwrite local land, labor, and memory.

Installation Components

  • Ürania Illusion Monitor
    A 3D-printed monitor shell containing a smartphone-based Pepper’s Ghost stage. The piece includes a slide-in phone tray, internal acrylic reflector, cutout figure, and LEDs that illuminate the hologram. The exterior is collaged with local newspaper articles on AI and “Project Blue” data-center development, treating the monitor as both domestic object and corporate shrine.
  • Project Blue Neck Sculpture
    A neck-like sculptural extension emerging from the monitor and wrapped in clippings about Project Blue and regional AI infrastructure. The form suggests a body caught between connection and constriction, linking data-center discourse to the human throat that carries voice and breath.
  • Smear Sites (photographic diptych)
    Two photographic prints of churches in Prague, printed as digital inkjet on the reverse side of glossy photo paper and mounted behind a laser-cut acrylic Gothic frame. The images are partially smeared and distorted, echoing architectural spires while questioning how “sacred” spaces—religious or technological—can be repurposed for extraction, myth-making, and reputational “smear” campaigns.
  • Prompt and title cards (video)
    On the hologram video track, a series of retro-UI title cards appear with questions about AI’s water use, social impact, and echo-chamber dynamics in the desert. These prompts are speculative by design and function as invitations rather than answers.

About the Project

Urania operates at the intersection of speculative fiction, domestic infrastructure, and environmental accounting. The project treats AI not as a sentient agent, but as a corporate system sustained by continuous energy input, human attention, and material extraction.
